--- layout: post title: green tea ice cream date: '2008-12-01T12:52:00.000-08:00' author: atpic tags: cooking modified_time: '2008-12-01T12:57:51.407-08:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-4344287556980631462.post-156585033344795946 blogger_orig_url: http://tuxcook.blogspot.com/2008/12/green-tea-ice-cream.html --- http://japanesefood.about.com/od/japanesedessertsweet/ss/makinggreenice.htm http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Green_tea_ice_cream Green Tea Powder (Matcha/Maccha) Ingredients: * 3/4 cup milk * 2 egg yolks * 5 tbsp sugar * 3/4 cup heavy cream * 1 tbsp maccha green tea powder * 3 tbsps hot water Preparation: Mix hot water and green tea powder together in a bowl and set aside. Lightly whisk egg yolks in a pan. Add sugar in the pan and mix well. Gradually add milk in the pan and mix well. Put the pan on low heat and heat the mixture, stirring constantly. When the mixture is thickened, remove the pan from the heat. Soak the bottom of the pan in ice water and cool the mixture. Add green tea in the egg mixture and mix well, cooling in ice water. Add whipped heavy cream in the mixture and stir gently. Pour the mixture in an ice cream maker and freeze, following instructions of the ice cream maker. Matcha is ground green tea. Steamed, cooled and dried green tea leaves are sorted into stems and leaves. The stems and veins are sorted out to leave the most tender part of the leaf - Tencha. The Tencha is then ground down to make 'Matcha'. It is a delicate process that takes a considerable amount of time. It is matcha tea that is used in the Japanese Tea Ceremony, and the best matcha is some of the most expensive tea in the world. Like other teas it is rich in antioxidants called polyphenols.
