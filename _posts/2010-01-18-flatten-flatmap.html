--- layout: post title: flatten, flatmap date: '2010-01-18T02:41:00.000-08:00' author: atpic tags: scala modified_time: '2010-01-18T02:45:41.884-08:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-6398118045441905973.post-7826204708805045454 blogger_orig_url: http://tuxscala.blogspot.com/2010/01/flatten-flatmap.html --- Use flatten from the List singleton object, which doesn't require that implicit parameter: 
scala> val foo = List(List(1), List("a","b","c"), List(2.3))
foo: List[List[Any]] = List(List(1), List(a, b, c), List(2.3))

scala> List.flatten(foo)
res7: List[Any] = List(1, a, b, c, 2.3)
http://blog.tmorris.net/youd-naturally-write-flatmap-yourself-if-asked-the-question/ flatten(list) is equivalent to list flatMap (x => x). Furthermore, this should hold for more than just list, but for other type constructors too (sadly, Option is missing a flatten function: Option[Option[A]] => Option[A]).
